Energy Olympics!

Imagine you are a lightbulb. How much power do you think it'll take you to supply light for a week without stopping? First off, what even is power?

In physics, power is the amount of work you do over a certain amount of time. It is typically measured in Watts, which is also used to measure the power usage of a lightbulb.

The amount of power you'll need depends on the type of lightbulb ou are. You may have heard of terms like "LED" or "60-Watt Incandescent," but do you know what they mean? In this game we're going to find out!

Step 1: Collecting data

First, you're going to do some exercise. Grab a stopwatch - we're going to record some values to figure out how much exercise you would have to do if you were a lightbulb!

Sprinting

It's a little hard to find space to sprint in the house - that's okay! All we need for this measurement is your weight in pounds.

Stair Climbing

Count the number of stairs in your staircase. Then time yourself running up the stairs as quickly — and as safely! — as you can. Record those values here.

Hurdle Jumping

Pick a number between 10 and 25 and time yourself jumping that amount of times. Record how many jumps you did and how long it took you here.
